When you trade in cryptocurrency markets, you're participating in a 24/7 global ecosystem where assets can be exchanged directly between participants without traditional financial intermediaries.

Centralized Exchanges (CEX)

Company-operated platforms that facilitate cryptocurrency trading while maintaining custody of user funds.

Structure

Company-operated platforms

Custody Model

Exchange holds user funds

Trading Speed

High (milliseconds)

Liquidity

Generally high

Security Model

Company security protocols

Example Platforms

Binance, Coinbase, Kraken

Did you know? Centralized exchanges handle the majority of global cryptocurrency trading volume, with the top exchanges processing billions of dollars in transactions daily.

Decentralized Exchanges (DEX)

Smart contract-based protocols that enable peer-to-peer trading without custodial intermediaries.

Structure

Smart contract-based protocols

Custody Model

Self-custody (users control keys)

Trading Speed

Moderate to slow (seconds/minutes)

Liquidity

Varies by protocol

Security Model

Blockchain consensus security

Example Platforms

Uniswap, dYdX, SushiSwap

Did you know? Decentralized exchanges operate without a central authority, using smart contracts to facilitate trades directly between users' wallets without requiring them to give up control of their private keys.

P2P Platforms

Direct user-to-user matching services that facilitate cryptocurrency trading between individuals without an order book.

Structure

Direct user-to-user matching

Custody Model

Self-custody during trading

Trading Speed

Slow (manual approvals)

Liquidity

Generally lower

Security Model

Escrow and reputation systems

Example Platforms

LocalBitcoins, Paxful

Did you know? P2P platforms often serve as crucial on-ramps in regions with limited banking infrastructure or regulatory challenges, enabling cryptocurrency adoption in underserved markets.

Key Cryptocurrency Market Facts

  • 24/7 Trading: Unlike traditional financial markets, cryptocurrency markets operate 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year.
  • Global Access: Crypto markets provide financial access to billions of people worldwide regardless of geographic location or banking status.
  • Market Cap: The total cryptocurrency market capitalization has grown from virtually zero in 2009 to over $1 trillion in recent years.
  • Regulation: Cryptocurrency regulation varies widely across jurisdictions, with some countries embracing innovation and others imposing significant restrictions.
